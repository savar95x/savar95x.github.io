My Workouts

This is a collection of all the workout routines I have followed, both the current one and the older ones.

Current

As of 20 Jan 2024

Priority

A lot of arms - 2 good sessions
A lot of shoulders - 2 good sessions
Good torso and legs - 2 good sessions but not necessary to be fresh

Exercise Selection

  • Chest - Incline Bench Press
  • Back - T Bar Row, Wide Pronated Pulldown, Cable Pullovers
  • Triceps - Skull Crushers Overhead
  • Biceps - EZ Bar / Barbell Bicep Curl
  • Front Delt - Smith Machine Overhead Press
  • Side Delt - Upright Egyptian Lateral Raise
  • Rear Delt - Reverse Pec Deck
  • Quads - High Bar Squat
  • Hamstring - Romanian Deadlift, Seated Leg Curl
  • Others - Standing Calf Raises, Decline Sit Ups

Not a lot of variation as I feel I don’t personally need it yet.

Split

Essentially a flexible Upper Lower split.

  • Incline Bench, T Bar Row Seated Cable Row, Smith Machine OHP, Pulldown, Pullover, Rev Pec Deck
  • Skull Crusher SS Bicep Curl, Squat, RDL
  • Rest
  • Incline Bench, T Bar Row Seated Cable Row, Smith Machine OHP, Pulldown, Pullover, Rev Pec Deck
  • Rest / Skip
  • Skull Crusher SS Bicep Curl, Squat, Leg Curl
  • Rest
  • Repeat

What I actually do

I follow evolving rep range, a method of unlimited hypertrophy, on almost every exercise.
If had the option, I would choose antagonistic supersets over training twice the number of times, but my college gym is pretty crowded, especially at this new year resolution time, hence no big supersets.

Day 1

Morning

  1. Incline Barbell Bench Press 5-10, 3-5 sets SS Standing Calf Raises

Evening

  1. Smith Machine OHP 6-12, 2-4 sets
  2. Cable Pullover 8-12, 1-2 sets
  3. Pulldown Wide Overhand 6-12, 2-4 sets
  4. Seated Cable Row 5-10, 2-4 sets
  5. Reverse Pec Deck 10+, 2-3 sets

Day 2

Morning

  1. Skull Crusher 6-12, 2-4 sets SS Bicep Curls 6-12, 2-4 sets

Evening

  1. Squat High Bar Normal-Close Stance 5-10, 3-4 sets
  2. Romanian Deadlift 5-10, 3-4 sets

Day 3

  • Rest. Important to not do anything physically (and ideally mentally as well) stressful.

Then Repeat, with the RDL being replaced with seated leg curl.
Sometimes I even take rest between day 1 and 2, just because I don’t need to train that much yet, so why not take free recovery days.

Lateral Raises are done 2 to 3 times per week, done on days when I feel they’re ready to train (usually train them either arms or legs), either in the beginning of the workout or the end.
Abs and Upper traps should be trained, but I’m lazy.
